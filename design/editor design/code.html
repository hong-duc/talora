Manuscript #402

The Alchemist's Silence

Draft Chapter One Last edited by The Archivist

The ink in the well did not just sit; it pulsed. Elias watched it under the dim amber glow of the Library’s floating lanterns. Outside the leaded glass windows, the void churned with silent, purple storms—the kind of weather that unmade memories and rewrote the histories of smaller men.

He dipped his quill. The nib, carved from the bone of a long-dead constellation, sang a high, vibrating note as it touched the vellum. This wasn't just storytelling. This was architecture. Each word was a stone laid in the foundation of a reality that didn't yet exist, but desperately needed to.

"In the beginning," he wrote, "there was only the whisper."

The whisper was older than the silence that followed it. It was the hum of the universe trying to remember its own name. Elias felt the cold crawl up his spine—the familiar touch of the Arcane. To write here, in the Whispering Library, was to invite the shadows to look over your shoulder.
